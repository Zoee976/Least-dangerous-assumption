What is the Least Dangerous Assumption?

The Least Dangerous Assumption (LDA) is a principle of inclusive education that emphasizes presuming competence in individuals with disabilities. Introduced by Anne Donnellan in 1984, LDA ensures students receive opportunities to succeed, fostering their potential and independence.

Why the Least Dangerous Assumption Matters

Presuming competence leads to:

  • Equity: Ensures fair access to education for all students.
  • Empowerment: Builds confidence and advocacy skills.
  • Inclusion: Promotes belonging and reduces stigma.
  • Long-term Benefits: Higher expectations improve future outcomes.

Applications in Special Education

Here are some ways LDA is applied in practice:

  • Creating inclusive classrooms with tailored supports.
  • Incorporating assistive technologies to enhance learning.
  • Encouraging participation in group activities.
  • Developing strength-based Individualized Education Plans (IEPs).
